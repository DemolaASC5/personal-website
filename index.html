Wind Turbine Data Analysis

Completed during internship at GE Renewable Energy

From June 2021 to August 2021, I served as a Technical Early Identification (EID) Intern at GE Renewable Energy. Here are some of the accomplishments from the internship:

  • Utilized Tableau 2020.2, Python, and SQL to upgrade dashboards that visualized data for over 40,000 GE turbines for the Operability, Fleet Reliability, and Productivity teams.
  • Created a new optimized dashboard with visualizations for fleet wide data and popular metadata channels that expanded the look back range by over 2000% and reduced storage by over 50%.
  • Created a framework for a highly optimized live Deep Dive Dashboard that covers all metadata channels and relevant statistics.

Equity in Renewable Energy

Completed for Cornell CEE 5420 Course

During the Spring 2021 semester, I enrolled in Professor Greeshma Gadikota's course, CEE 5420: Energy Technologies & Subsurface Resources. My final project featured a deep dive on energy justice by evaluating the state of equity in the renewable energy sector as of 2021. This paper and project served as the root of my passion of connecting social justice, environmental sustaianbility through engineering and technology. The paper can be found here.

Urban Heat Island Modeling

Completed for Cornell BEE 4750 Course

During the Fall 2022 semester, I enrolled in Professor Anderson's course, BEE 4750: Environmental Systems Analysis. My final project featured an Urban Heat Island modeling simulation of a city that mirrored Washington D.C. with a technological and economical analysis for methods of reducing the Urban Heat Island effect. The project was completed in Julia with Anna Cliche and Cormac Mahoney and the GitHub repo can be found here.

Carbon Footprint Calculator

Cornell GreenClub

After developing proficiency in the Django framework and applying my HTML, CSS, and JavaScript skills as a Lead Web Developer for Cornell's GreenClub carbon offset purchasing service, I took on the opportunity to become the GreenClub Team Lead and lead the charge for developing a carbon footprint calculator that is specialized to the Cornell local community. We hope that this carbon footprint calculator will enable the Cornell community to understand their enviornmental impact and hope that GreenClub as an organization will help students be the most enviornmentally sustainable version of themselves.

Create an account on the GreenClub website to recieve updates on the model!

Currents Smart HVAC

Cornell University Sustainable Design (CUSD) Currents Project

CUSD's Currents provides a robust and specialized method for improving the efficiency of heating, ventilation, and air-conditioning (HVAC) systems. As a technology, Currents enables users to set their optimal room temperatures, other data points, and their calendar to optimize the heating and cooling of office spaces, hotel rooms, and more.

As a Lead of the Computer Science subteam, I focus on ensuring the integration between the technical components work as intended. I specifically focus on the Mobile & Server components and utilize skills in JavaScript, PostgreSQL, and Python to pass data to prediction, algorithms, and thermal models to help set temperature points.

The project is currently still in development with a goal of being implemented in Cornell's Mechanical & Aerospace Engineering Building, Upson Hall, in 2023.
